Week 1:  What an LLM actually is ?
-	From the Materials : 
	Article 1 : A language model like GeneGPT becomes more reliable when it can access external tools such as databases or APIs to retrieve information, rather than relying solely on its internal memory. Instead of guessing or making assumptions, it fetches accurate and up-to-date data in real time, which reduces errors and improves the precision of its responses.
	Article 2 : Integrating human knowledge into neural networks helps make models more efficient, more accurate, and better suited to complex scientific problems.
	Article 3 : Some models can achieve good apparent performance without actually learning the correct features of the data. They may rely instead on misleading correlations or spurious cues rather than the true relevant factors.

-	Surprises 
	I asked how to find a dog in an image. ChatGPT gave a general description of dogs instead of really looking at an image. When I asked Antigravity the same question, it said the model might use wrong clues and think it found a dog even if it is not sure or correct.

Week 2:  Agentic IDEs on bioinformatics tasks
-	From the Materials : 
	Article 1 : The idea of ReAct is particularly powerful because it addresses an important limitation of language models. A standalone LLM can often reason correctly in theory, but still make mistakes or even generate false information when it lacks reliable data. With ReAct, the model first structures its thinking through reasoning, then takes action by retrieving real information from sources such as APIs, the web, or databases, and finally refines or corrects its reasoning based on the information it obtains.

-	Surprises : 
	AI can generate false but convincing information. For example, when asked “What is the OpenAI support phone number?”, it may produce a fictional number like +1 800 123 4567,which can mislead users into believing it is real.
